1. Know


Regardless of work, I immediately want to know who my users and clientele are. Everything from their background to initial needs, I observe, adapt, and walk a mile in their shoes to lay the foundation for the work.

2. Define


Any existing or potential problems my client is facing, I want to know about them. Designers are ultimately problem solvers, so the more of them to uncover, the better the work will turn out.

3. Ideate


Actively brainstorm and come up with a variety of solutions to fit my client's needs. From there, narrow down to the best possible solution. Active collaboration on ideas is crucial here.

4. Build


From rough hand-drawn sketches, to wireframes, to pixel perfect mock-ups, the design solutions come to life. Everything is shared, commented on, and improved upon, constantly.

5. Measure


A mentor of mine once said "everything is a draft." Once the design is pushed, published, or sent off, never put it back down. Measure and test to look for any ways to improve it through feedback and metrics.

6. Iterate


Designs never reach their highest potential the first time around. Always go back and reengage clients with new ideas and solutions, whether it uncovers and solves an existing problem or just needs to look better.
