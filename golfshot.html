Golfshot

Brand / UI and UX / Front End

Branding, web, and mobile design for a prominent golf GPS, scoring, and tee time booking app.

With Golfers in Mind

In 2009, Golfshot was dreamt up as a handy utility for golfers, used simply to glance at distances. Over time we realized that it had far greater potential in improving the golfer's experience, on and off the course.

This product truly tested our design thinking and forced us to improve our learning and development processes. Golfers are a tough crowd, but a vocal one at that. After many moons of gathering qualitatives, measuring data, defining pain points, story-boarding and IA, and executing action items, there was nothing more fulfilling than seeing increased adoption and retainment.

Golf can be a leisurely, yet competitive sport for players of all ages. Our goal was to create a product that not only complimented the golfer's mindset while playing, but also prevented from interrupting it, a tall challenge that I feel we accomplished and can grow upon.

Design to the Core

Thought-out design principles, constant iteration, and "throwing rocks" at each other's work is the core of building and changing relationships with your users.

A cornerstone in improving Golfshot, not just the app, but also brand and marketing collateral that millions encounter day to day, is product agnosticism. We feel that every idea, project, or file we touch should have our persona's intentions in mind: as a golfer, I want a product and brand I can understand and trust with my game, so that I can continue to develop as an individual player.

To accomplish this, we had to build and iterate on our design system, a process of creating the very essense of our design language, crafting metaphors, and applying them to resusable pieces and components for all types of mediums.
