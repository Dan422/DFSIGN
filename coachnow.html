CoachNow

Brand / UI and UX / Front End

Branding, web, and mobile design for a communication and analysis app for coaches.

From Clutter to Communication

In 2013, CoachNow was built to help golf coaches. The initial problem it strived to solve: remove clutter from their process. However over time, the problem became much more clear: as a coach, how do I just improve my communication with athletes so they can engage and retain content?

Once we started to branch into other sports and empathizing with users to craft personas, we quickly realized that our audience was very diverse and used many different tactics of coaching. To adapt, we had to really rethink many user journeys and build features that coaches are familiar with day-to-day.

We gathered every bit of qualitative feedback through conversations, focus groups, written reviews, and NPS surverys to get a better gage of what our next approach was. The result? Real answers from real people that shifted our way of thinking and inspired us to build and iterate CoachNow to this day.

Multiple Hats

During a feedback session, I had the pleasure of talking with a prominent figure in the youth basketball space. He told me that all coaches wear multiple hats, and even with new platforms or tools, they're always performing many jobs for athletes, parents, and alike.

We also learned that there's nothing like the coaching environment, seeing real-time results and satisfaction in a dedicated space for growing relationships, amidst all the chaos.

With our new design methodology, principles, and system we wanted to capture the essence of this space and results with the goal of building designs coaches of many backgrounds and traits can appreciate and relate with.
